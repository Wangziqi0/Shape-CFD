\textbf{Cross-first-stage disentanglement (5-corpus BGE-M3 replication).} To separate the rerank-quality effect from the first-stage effect, we ran the same Qwen3-8B-Q4 listwise reranker on \textbf{BGE-M3 cosine top-100} (the same first-stage as BGE-reranker-v2-m3 in Table~\ref{tab:agent-b-baselines}) across all 5 BEIR corpora using the audited-rebuild BGE-M3 vectors (see \S\ref{subsec:cross-model} \textit{Vector-provenance note}; zero-rate 0.000\% on NFCorpus/SciFact, 0.066\% on FiQA). Results are summarised in Table~\ref{tab:llm-listwise-bgem3}. The pattern is sharply task-dependent: listwise reranking improves NDCG@10 on NFCorpus ($+6.97\%$ rel-to-cosine), SciFact ($+2.28\%$), and SCIDOCS ($+13.16\%$); but degrades on ArguAna ($-19.53\%$) and FiQA ($-5.81\%$). The \emph{direction} of the ArguAna degradation is robust to first-stage upgrade: on Qwen3 token-centroid first-stage (Table~\ref{tab:llm-listwise}) listwise hurt ArguAna by $-17.1\%$; on the stronger BGE-M3 first-stage it still hurts by $-19.5\%$, indicating that the failure mode is not first-stage-induced but a property of the listwise prompt-rerank operator itself on argument-style queries. The result also clarifies the previous Table~\ref{tab:llm-listwise} ``vs.\ BGE-rerank $-9.5\%$'' on Qwen-centroid first-stage: a substantial part of that gap, but not all of it, was first-stage induced. Implementation: \texttt{benchmark/llm\_rerank\_bench\_bge.py --vectors\_prefix bge\_m3\_ --results\_suffix bge\_m3\_first\_stage\_5corpora\_v2}; full output: \texttt{benchmark/data/results/llm\_rerank\_results\_bge\_m3\_first\_stage\_5corpora\_v2.json}.

The honest finding (5-corpus BGE-M3 first-stage, Table~\ref{tab:llm-listwise-bgem3}) is that generic LLM listwise reranking is not a universal improvement, even on a strong (retrieval-trained) first-stage: it can substantially help (NFCorpus $+6.97\%$, SCIDOCS $+13.16\%$), be near-neutral (SciFact $+2.28\%$), or sharply hurt (ArguAna $-19.53\%$, FiQA $-5.81\%$). Across these five datasets, our pipeline (Table~\ref{tab:agent-b-baselines}: NFCorpus 0.327, SciFact 0.495, ArguAna 0.442, SCIDOCS 0.215, FiQA 0.398) outperforms LLM-listwise on \emph{every} dataset, with the largest absolute margins on ArguAna ($+0.176$ NDCG@10) and FiQA ($+0.018$ NDCG@10). This is consistent with prior observations that generic LLMs lack the retrieval-specific calibration of trained cross-encoders, and additionally suggests that the failure modes (argument-style queries on ArguAna; financial-QA queries on FiQA) survive a first-stage upgrade.

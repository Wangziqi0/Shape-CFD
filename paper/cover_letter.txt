Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Documents Are Shapes, Not Points: Training-Free Retrieval via PQ-Chamfer Distance and Graph Smoothing" for consideration for publication in Information Processing and Management.

This paper introduces a training-free geometric post-processing pipeline for dense document retrieval. The core idea is to treat LLM hidden states as point clouds rather than collapsing them into single vectors. We propose PQ-Chamfer distance—a new dissimilarity measure that repurposes Product Quantization from a compression tool into a distance metric—and combine it with graph Laplacian smoothing for score refinement.

Key results:
- The pipeline improves retrieval quality by +2.7% to +136.2% (NDCG@10) across six BEIR benchmarks, spanning 2,500 to 523,000 documents.
- On two benchmarks (FiQA and SCIDOCS), the training-free pipeline surpasses BGE-large-en-v1.5, a model specifically trained for retrieval.
- Cross-model experiments on three embedding architectures (Qwen3-8B, BGE-M3, BGE-large) confirm the method is model-agnostic.
- We report 21 systematically falsified approaches, establishing clear applicability boundaries.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by another journal. A preprint version is available on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19402100).

We believe this work is well-suited for IPM given its focus on novel information processing methods with strong empirical validation across multiple datasets and models.

Thank you for your consideration.

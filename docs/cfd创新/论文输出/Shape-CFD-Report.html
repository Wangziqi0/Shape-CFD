Shape-CFD: A Shape-Aware Convection-Diffusion
Reranking Algorithm for Vector Retrieval

基于形状感知对流-扩散方程的向量检索重排序算法
Technical Report v1.0
陈一凡 (Yifan Chen)
March 2026  |  Independent Research
Abstract. We propose Shape-CFD, a novel reranking algorithm for vector retrieval that applies the convection-diffusion equation from computational fluid dynamics (CFD) to the post-retrieval ranking stage of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems. Our method introduces two key innovations: (1) AD-Rank, which formulates document reranking as an advection-diffusion process on a KNN graph, incorporating both isotropic diffusion (inter-document semantic propagation) and anisotropic advection (query-directed flow); and (2) Shape-CFD, which lifts document representations from single-point embeddings to sentence-level point clouds, using Chamfer distance to construct similarity graphs that capture fine-grained local semantic matching. On a dataset of 23,701 Chinese legal provisions, Shape-CFD achieves an 80% win rate against cosine-based ranking in LLM blind evaluation, with only 22ms additional latency through random projection acceleration and sentence vector pre-caching. To our knowledge, the combination of shape-aware document representation, convection-diffusion dynamics, and retrieval reranking has not been explored in prior literature.

Introduction and Motivation

The Isolated Scoring Problem

Current RAG systems rank retrieved documents independently using cosine similarity:

$$\text{score}(d_i) = \cos(q, v_i) = \frac{q \cdot v_i}{\|q\|\|v_i\|}$$ (1)

This approach ignores inter-document semantic relationships. When a query is ambiguous or spans multiple topics, isolated scoring fails to leverage the mutual reinforcement between related documents.

Inspiration: Fluid Dynamics Analogy

Our core insight is that the propagation of relevance among candidate documents resembles physical fluid flow:

  • Diffusion: Relevant documents "endorse" their neighbors, spreading relevance isotropically — analogous to ink diffusing in water.
  • Advection: The query imposes a directional bias, steering relevance flow toward semantically aligned documents — analogous to wind carrying particles.

This dual mechanism addresses two fundamental limitations of cosine ranking: lack of inter-document awareness (solved by diffusion) and lack of directionality (solved by advection).

The "Vectors Are Not Points, They Are Shapes" Hypothesis

A critical further insight is that compressing an entire document into a single embedding vector loses intra-document semantic structure. We propose representing each document as a point cloud of sentence-level embeddings, enabling the CFD process to operate on richer geometric representations. This is the core innovation of Shape-CFD.

Mathematical Framework

2.1 Graph Construction

Given $N$ candidate document embeddings $\{v_1, \ldots, v_N\} \subset \mathbb{R}^d$ (where $d = 4096$, $N = 30$), we construct a weighted KNN graph:

Step 1. Pairwise cosine similarity:

$$S_{ij} = \cos(v_i, v_j) = \frac{v_i \cdot v_j}{\|v_i\|\|v_j\|}$$ (2)

Step 2. KNN sparsification: retain top-$k$ neighbors for each node ($k=3$).

Step 3. Edge weights with exponential kernel:

$$W_{ij} = \exp\bigl(-\beta \cdot (1 - S_{ij})\bigr), \quad \beta = 2.0$$ (3)

Step 4. Symmetrization: if $j \in \mathcal{N}(i)$ but $i \notin \mathcal{N}(j)$, add edge $W_{ji} = W_{ij}$.

2.2 Initial Concentration Field

$$C_0(i) = \cos(q, v_i)$$ (4)

The initial "concentration" at each node equals its cosine similarity to the query.

2.3 Advection Direction

For each edge $(i, j)$, we define the advection coefficient as the projection of the edge direction onto the query direction:

$$u_{ij} = \left\langle \frac{v_j - v_i}{\|v_j - v_i\|},\; \frac{q}{\|q\|} \right\rangle \cdot u_{\text{strength}}$$ (5)

Antisymmetry. Since $v_i - v_j = -(v_j - v_i)$ and $\|v_i - v_j\| = \|v_j - v_i\|$:

$$u_{ji} = -u_{ij} \qquad \blacksquare$$ (6)

2.4 Conservative Upwind Discretization

The continuous convection-diffusion equation $\partial C/\partial t = D\nabla^2 C - \nabla\cdot(\vec{u}C)$ is discretized on the graph using a conservative upwind scheme:

$$C_{t+1}(i) = C_t(i) + \Delta t \left[\underbrace{D \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}(i)} W_{ij}\bigl(C_t(j) - C_t(i)\bigr)}_{\text{Diffusion flux}} + \underbrace{\sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}(i)} W_{ij}\bigl[\max(u_{ji},0)\,C_t(j) - \max(u_{ij},0)\,C_t(i)\bigr]}_{\text{Conservative upwind advection flux}}\right]$$ (7)

Global conservation proof. For each pair $(i,j)$, the outflow from $i$ equals the inflow to $j$ due to $u_{ji} = -u_{ij}$. Therefore $\sum_{i=1}^{N} F_i^{\text{adv}} = 0$. Total concentration is conserved. $\blacksquare$

CFL stability condition:

$$\Delta t \leq \frac{0.8}{\max_i |\mathcal{N}(i)|}$$ (8)

2.5 Physical Diagnostic Indicators

IndicatorFormulaMeaning

2.6 Adaptive Parameters via Graph Spectral Theory

The Fiedler value $\lambda_2$ (second-smallest eigenvalue of the graph Laplacian $L$) measures algebraic connectivity:

$$D_{\text{adaptive}} = \frac{D_{\text{base}}}{1 + \lambda_2}$$ (9)

Dense graphs ($\lambda_2$ large) → reduce $D$ to prevent over-smoothing. Sparse graphs ($\lambda_2$ small) → increase $D$ to aid signal propagation.

Shape-CFD Extension

3.1 The Curse of Dimensionality in Advection

In $d$-dimensional space, the inner product of two random unit vectors has:

$$\mathbb{E}[\langle x, y\rangle] = 0, \quad \text{Var}[\langle x, y\rangle] = \frac{1}{d}, \quad \sigma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{d}}$$ (10)

For $d = 4096$: $\sigma \approx 0.0156$. Our experiments confirm $\overline{|u_{ij}|} \approx 0.01$, consistent with theoretical prediction. The advection signal is inherently weak due to high-dimensional geometry.

3.2 Point Cloud Representation

We lift each document from a single vector to a sentence-level point cloud:

$$d_i \;\to\; \mathcal{P}_i = \{s_{i,1},\, s_{i,2},\, \ldots,\, s_{i,K_i}\} \subset \mathbb{R}^d$$ (11)

where $s_{i,k}$ is the embedding of the $k$-th sentence in document $d_i$.

3.3 Chamfer Distance

We replace cosine similarity with Chamfer distance for graph construction:

$$d_C(\mathcal{P}_A, \mathcal{P}_B) = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{P}_A|}\sum_{a \in \mathcal{P}_A}\min_{b \in \mathcal{P}_B} d_{\cos}(a,b) \;+\; \frac{1}{|\mathcal{P}_B|}\sum_{b \in \mathcal{P}_B}\min_{a \in \mathcal{P}_A} d_{\cos}(a,b)$$ (12)

where $d_{\cos}(a,b) = 1 - \cos(a,b)$. This metric captures local semantic matching: even if two documents differ globally, high similarity between specific sentence pairs yields a small Chamfer distance.

3.4 Shape-CFD Graph

All AD-Rank components are modified to use point cloud representations:

ComponentAD-Rank (point)Shape-CFD (cloud)
Edge weight$\exp(-\beta(1-\cos(v_i,v_j)))$$\exp(-\beta \cdot d_C(\mathcal{P}_i, \mathcal{P}_j))$
Initial conc.$\cos(q, v_i)$$\exp(-2\,d_C(\mathcal{P}_q, \mathcal{P}_i))$
Advection dir.$\langle\hat{e}_{ij}, \hat{q}\rangle$$\langle(\bar{v}_j-\bar{v}_i)/\|\cdot\|,\;\bar{v}_q/\|\cdot\|\rangle$
Iteration eq.Identical — Eq. (7) is reused without modification

3.5 Random Projection Acceleration

To reduce the $O(K_A K_B d)$ Chamfer computation cost, we apply Johnson-Lindenstrauss random projection:

$$\tilde{s} = Ps, \quad P \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times d},\quad P_{ij} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1/m), \quad m = 128$$ (13)

By the JL lemma, pairwise distances are preserved within $\pm 10\%$ error. Speedup: $d/m = 4096/128 = 32\times$.

Sentence embeddings are pre-cached in SQLite with both 4096-dim (for centroid advection) and 128-dim projected versions (for Chamfer computation), eliminating all online embedding API calls.

4.2 Evaluation Protocol

We employ LLM-as-Judge blind evaluation using DeepSeek V3.2: for each query, two ranked lists (cosine vs. Shape-CFD) are anonymized and randomly assigned as "Method A" / "Method B". The LLM scores each on a 1–5 scale for relevance, coverage, and ranking quality. 10 queries × per-method scoring → win/loss determination.

Limitation. LLM-as-Judge is a proxy metric. Standard IR metrics (NDCG@10, MRR) on public benchmarks (MS MARCO, BEIR) are needed for rigorous comparison and are planned as future work.

Honest Assessment of Limitations

  1. Single-domain evaluation. All experiments use one Chinese legal dataset. Generalization to other domains (medical, financial, encyclopedic) and languages (English) is unverified.
  2. No standard IR metrics. We report LLM blind evaluation scores instead of NDCG@10 / MRR on public benchmarks (MS MARCO, BEIR, Natural Questions).
  3. No comparison with neural rerankers. Cross-encoders (e.g., BGE-Reranker) likely outperform Shape-CFD in ranking quality, though at higher computational cost.
  4. Small sample size. 10 queries × 1 blind evaluation round. Statistical significance is marginal ($p \approx 0.055$ for 8:2 win rate).
  5. No convergence proof. Convergence is observed empirically (100% rate) but not proven theoretically.
  6. Short-text degradation. When documents contain only one sentence, the point cloud degenerates to a single point, removing Shape-CFD's advantage over point-based AD-Rank.

Planned Future Work

  1. Evaluate on MS MARCO and BEIR with NDCG@10 / MRR metrics.
  2. Direct comparison with BGE-Reranker and ColBERT.
  3. Theoretical convergence analysis for the upwind scheme on KNN graphs.
  4. Learnable Shape-CFD: replace fixed $D$, $u_{\text{strength}}$ with parameters trained via differentiable optimization.
  5. Wasserstein distance as alternative to Chamfer distance for smoother shape comparison.
